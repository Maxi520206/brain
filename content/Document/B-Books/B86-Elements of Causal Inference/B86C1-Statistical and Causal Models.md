### 1.1 Probability Theory and Statistics

- **Probability Space | 概率空间**: A mathematical structure $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, P)$ where $\Omega$ is the set of outcomes, $\mathcal{F}$ is a collection of events, and $P$ is a probability measure.
- **Statistical Learning | 统计学习**: The inverse problem of probability theory; it aims to infer properties of the underlying mathematical structure from experimental outcomes.
- **i.i.d. Assumption | 独立同分布假设**: The assumption that observations $(x_1, y_1), \dots, (x_n, y_n)$ are realizations of random variables generated independently by the same random experiment.
- **Violations**: The i.i.d. assumption is violated when distributions shift or interventions occur, both of which are linked to causality.

---

### 1.2 Learning Theory

- **Two Levels of Ill-posed-ness**:
    1. **Statistical**: A finite sample cannot contain all information about the underlying distribution.
    2. **Causal**: Complete knowledge of an observational distribution usually does not determine the underlying causal model.
- **Risk | 风险**: In binary classification, the expected error to be minimized is defined as $R[f] = \int \frac{1}{2} |f(x) - y| dP_{X,Y}(x,y)$.
- **Universal Consistency | 通用一致性**: The property of a learning algorithm (e.g., SVMs or nearest neighbor) to converge to the lowest achievable risk as data approaches infinity.
- **Learning Rates**: For any algorithm, there exist specific problems for which learning rates from finite data are arbitrarily slow.
- **Standard ML Requirement**: Traditional machine learning assumes the underlying distribution remains fixed between training and testing.

---

### 1.3 Causal Modeling and Learning

- **Causal Reasoning | 因果推理**: The process of drawing conclusions from a causal model, specifically analyzing effects of interventions or distribution changes.
- **Causal Learning | 因果学习**: The process of inferring causal structures from empirical implications, including observational data, data under interventions, or distribution changes.
- **Structure Identifiability | 结构可辨识性**: The question of which parts of a causal structure can be inferred in principle from a joint distribution.
- **Reichenbach’s Common Cause Principle | 赖兴巴赫共同原因原则**: A statistical dependence between variables $X$ and $Y$ indicates that $X$ causes $Y$, $Y$ causes $X$, or they share a common cause (confounder) $Z$.

### 1.4 Two Examples

### 1.4.1 Pattern Recognition

- **SCM Comparison**: Two different Structural Causal Models (SCMs) can lead to the same joint distribution $P_{X,Y}$ but differ interventionally.
    
    <aside> 💡
    
    两个不同的因果世界，可能产生完全一样的数据分布
    
    但是：
    
    如果你进行 **干预 (intervention)**
    
    它们会产生 **不同结果**。
    
    </aside>
    
- **Model (i)**: Labels $Y$ are provided to a human who then produces images $X$.
    
    - Y → human → X
- **Model (ii)**: An intention $Z$ determines both the class to write $Y$ and the produced image $X$
    
    - Z → Y Z → X
- **Structural Causal Models (SCMs) | 结构因果模型**: A model where dependences are generated by functions representing physical mechanisms, treated as assignments rather than algebraic equations.
    
- **Abstraction of Time**: Causal and probabilistic structures often arise through processes in time, but time is frequently disregarded to create instructive abstractions.
